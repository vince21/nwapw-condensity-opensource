About

Condensity is the project of Vincent Wilson, Toby Ueno and Spencer Chang for the 2020 Northwest Advanced Programming Workshop. Condensity is a novel extraction-based summarization algorithm that harnesses a diverse assortment of natural language processing techniques. The algorithm works by first scoring sentences by synset word frequency. Then Condensity applies other optimized factors to the summarization such as sentiment analysis, Levenshtein similarity and word vectorization. The result is an algorithm that summarizes text to a high degree of accuracy and fidelity to key ideas. You can find our MIT licensed open source code here.

The creators of Condensity do not profit from this service and have intended it to be an educational tool protected under Fair Use. Please contact us at condensity.nwapw@gmail.com if you have any questions regarding the material published on this website.
